# Me
Hi there, I'm Bianca Corrêa! I'm a [travesti](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Travesti_(gender_identity)) and use she/her pronouns. 
I'm a Software Engineer with a B.Sc in Computer Science at [IC-UFF](https://www.ic.uff.br/ciencia-da-computacao/). This site contains my thoughts about computer science, engineering leadership, software engineering and infrastructure.

# Career
## IBM
I started my career at IBM in 2019 as an Software Engineer Intern and was later hired as an Application Developer within the IBM Global Financing (IGF) business unit, in IBM's Rio de Janeiro office.

Our team helped Business Operations teams in North America and LATAM (e.g. Contracts, Pricing, Billing) by modernizing and automating sensitive financial and accounting processes that were still stuck with 1950s technology (and some of them are *still* stuck 😅).

We did this by building internal web apps and web crawlers using [Vue.js](https://vuejs.org) and [Node.js](https://nodejs.org), which ran on IBM's [OpenShift-based](https://www.redhat.com) internal cloud. 
The crawlers ran within Linux & Windows VMs provided by IBM's Automation CoE team, which seamlessly integrated with the company's infrastructure (which is insanely huge!).

Together, we helped save over 10,000+ hours of manual work each year in just 5 countries. We then went on to expand this solution to every country IGF operates on –– if I recall correctly, by the time I left we had already expanded to SEA, EU, Oceania, Japan and China.

My pet project was an API used to store and retrieve the secrets and password that crawlers used to login into internal web pages and perform actions on behalf of an operator. It included a Vue.js UI backed by a Node.js BFF that allowed end-users to manage their secrets and passwords without calling the API directly. By the end, we applied for the team's second patent, but failed to get one as IBM was focusing on AI & ML patents at the time.

I learned a lot about information security and cryptography during this work, as the company deals with critical, highly sensitive infrastructure for the world's largest banks, governments, airlines, and healthcare systems ("invisible but everywhere").
